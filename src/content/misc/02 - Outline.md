### Disclaimer

This is a satirical course outline meant for humor and critique, not financial advice. It lampoons common marketing tropes around crypto and binary options while mimicking course structure and copy.

---

### Course Concept

**Title**: Crypto QuickRich Academy: From Zero to Moon in 7 Days
**Tagline**: Learn to trade like a legend; results may vary, mostly in memes.
**Tone**: Deadpan sales-course sincerity with wink-of-iron yuks, sly puns, and obvious-but-funny exaggerations.
**Target Audience**: People who have seen one too many late-night ads and want to laugh while learning what not to do.

---

### Module Structure and Lesson Map

#### Module 0 Onboarding Primer

- **Welcome Ritual** — “Claim your free NFT badge” (a static PNG called a badge) to trigger reciprocity.
- **Fast Win** — 3-minute quiz that tells you you’re already a trader; micro-commitment achieved.
- **Expectation Setting** — “You will either become rich or learn a valuable life lesson.”

#### Module 1 The Hype Primer

- **Lesson 1.1 Pump Psychology** — How FOMO sounds like a motivational speaker.
- **Lesson 1.2 Scarcity Tactics 101** — Limited seats, limited time, limited sense.
- **Satirical Exercise** — Write a fake countdown timer that says “Only 9 spots left” even when there are 900.

#### Module 2 The Tools and the Tinsel

- **Lesson 2.1 Wallets and Wizards** — Choosing a wallet based on how shiny the logo is.
- **Lesson 2.2 Exchanges and Their Terms** — Read the fine print aloud for comedic effect.
- **Practical Task** — Create a demo account and name it “Lambo Fund.”

#### Module 3 Binary Options for the Bold

- **Lesson 3.1 Binary Basics** — Heads or tails, but with charts.
- **Lesson 3.2 The Art of Betting Like a Pro** — How to sound confident while guessing.
- **Role Play** — Pitch a binary option to a friend using only metaphors about weather.

#### Module 4 Chart Porn and Pattern Worship

- **Lesson 4.1 Candlesticks and Constellations** — Read charts like horoscopes.
- **Lesson 4.2 Backtest Bingo** — Find patterns that would have worked last Tuesday.
- **Micro-commitment** — Post your “favorite pattern” in the community and get a badge.

#### Module 5 Funnels, Funnels, Funnels

- **Lesson 5.1 Webinar Wizardry** — How to combine urgency, authority, and a freebie PDF.
- **Lesson 5.2 Tripwires and Upsells** — Sell the course, then sell the “advanced” course about selling the course.
- **Copy Lab** — Rewrite a real ad into a satirical version that reveals the mechanics.

#### Module 6 Risk Reversal and Refund Theater

- **Lesson 6.1 Guarantees That Guarantee Nothing** — “Double-your-money promise” explained as theater.
- **Lesson 6.2 Refund Scripts** — How to politely say no when someone asks for money back.
- **Final Assignment** — Draft an honest refund policy that no marketer would use.

#### Module 7 Graduation and Exit Offers

- **Capstone Project** — Build a mock funnel that would make a cartoon villain proud.
- **Alumni Upsell** — Invite to the “Mastermind” where you pay more to learn how to pay less.
- **Certificate** — Printable PDF that says “Certified in Confident Speculation.”

### Satirical Marketing Funnel and Copy Examples

- **Lead Magnet**: “5 Secrets Exchanges Don’t Want You to Know” — includes obvious tips like “read the fees.”
- **Webinar Hook**: “How I Turned $50 into a Story” — urgency line: “Seats close when the moon aligns.”
- **Email Nurture Sequence**:
  - **Email 1**: Welcome and free checklist titled **“Moonshot or Moonwalk”**.
  - **Email 3**: Testimonial montage with obviously staged before/after photos and punny names.
  - **Cart Close Email**: “Doors close at midnight. Or whenever the market corrects. Same thing.”

**Sample One-Liners for Sales Pages**

- “Trade smarter, not harder; or at least trade with better lighting.”
- “Limited-time bonus: a template for writing urgent-sounding emails.”
- “Join 10,000 students and one guy who still thinks Bitcoin is a new app.”

### Learning Design and Delivery Techniques

- **Micro-Lessons**: 7–12 minute videos labeled as “snackable alpha.”
- **Active Tasks**: Meme-creation assignment to encourage sharing and social proof.
- **Progress Mechanics**: Progress bar that fills with gold coins and occasional confetti.
- **Community Hooks**: “Wins of the Week” thread where students post screenshots of imaginary profits.
- **Quizzes**: Multiple-choice questions with one obviously correct answer and three marketing-approved alternatives.

### Ethical Framing and Final Notes

- **Meta Lesson**: Teach students to spot persuasion techniques so they can resist them in real life.
- **Responsible Humor**: Use satire to expose manipulation, not to encourage reckless behavior.
- **Closing Line**: “If you enjoyed this course, please consider donating to your local financial literacy program.”
